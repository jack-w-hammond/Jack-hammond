The Outsiders Guide Vol.3: Soaked Film

The Outsiders Guide is a digital magazine at the centre of a community of bold young adults who have an insatiable curiosity. We explore the secrets of science and nature, going to the places you can't. We travel to the strangest landscapes on earth and meet the most elusive human beings, telling their stories through immersive visual journalism.

For the third Issue of our series, we have decided to take the reader on a different kind of journey. The Outsiders Guide Vol.3 explores how soaking 35mm camera film in different household chemicals and carcinogens can give strange and random effects on photos, whilst learning how you can achieve the same results and create unique images for yourself. This series is supposed to look like a ‘cookbook’ for experimental photography.

Submerging your camera film in liquid might not seem like a good idea, but when done properly its a photography technique that can garnish unexpectedly beautiful results. Without any post-processing work, you can get a distorted effect with vivid streaks of color and interesting textures.

These experiments are sometime called “film soups”. They should be done with caution because the process has a risk of completely destroying your photographs. We have created this step by step instruction book to help with the fundamentals of soaking film. We hope that it helps eliminate the destruction of your photographs and provides a foundation of knowledge to get you started with your own film soaking experimentations.
